# BYLAWS OF {{company.legal_name|upper}}
### (a Delaware corporation)

## ARTICLE I — OFFICES

**1.1 Registered Office.** The registered office of {{company.legal_name}} (the "Corporation") in Delaware shall be as set forth in the Certificate of Incorporation. **1.2 Other Offices.** The Corporation may have other offices, within or outside Delaware, as the Board of Directors (the "Board") determines.

## ARTICLE II — STOCKHOLDERS

**2.1 Annual Meeting.** An annual meeting of stockholders for the election of directors and any other proper business shall be held at a date, time and place (or by remote communication) designated by the Board.

**2.2 Special Meetings.** Special meetings of stockholders may be called at any time by the Board, the Chairman, or the Chief Executive Officer.

**2.3 Notice.** Written notice of each meeting shall be given not less than 10 nor more than 60 days before the meeting to each stockholder entitled to vote.

**2.4 Quorum.** The holders of a majority of the voting power of the shares entitled to vote, present in person or by proxy, shall constitute a quorum.

**2.5 Voting.** Except as otherwise required by law, the Certificate of Incorporation or these Bylaws, all matters other than the election of directors shall be decided by the affirmative vote of a majority of the voting power present. Directors shall be elected by a plurality of the votes cast.

**2.6 Action by Written Consent.** Any action required or permitted to be taken at a meeting of stockholders may be taken without a meeting by written consent signed by the holders of outstanding stock having not less than the minimum number of votes necessary to authorize such action at a meeting, in accordance with Section 228 of the DGCL.

**2.7 Record Date.** The Board may fix a record date not more than 60 nor less than 10 days before any meeting of stockholders.

## ARTICLE III — DIRECTORS

**3.1 Number.** The number of directors shall be fixed from time to time by resolution of the Board, and shall initially be {{directors_count}}.

**3.2 Term; Vacancies.** Each director holds office until the next annual meeting and until a successor is elected and qualified, or until earlier resignation or removal. Vacancies and newly created directorships may be filled by a majority of directors then in office.

**3.3 Meetings; Notice.** Regular meetings may be held without notice at times fixed by the Board. Special meetings may be called by the Chairman, the CEO, or any director on at least 24 hours' notice.

**3.4 Quorum; Action.** A majority of the total number of directors shall constitute a quorum. The act of a majority of directors present at a meeting at which a quorum is present shall be the act of the Board.

**3.5 Action by Written Consent.** Any action required or permitted at any meeting of the Board or a committee may be taken without a meeting if all members consent thereto in writing or by electronic transmission.

**3.6 Committees.** The Board may designate one or more committees, each consisting of one or more directors.

**3.7 Compensation.** Directors may receive such compensation for their services as the Board determines.

## ARTICLE IV — OFFICERS

**4.1 Officers.** The officers of the Corporation shall include a Chief Executive Officer and a Secretary, and may include a President, Chief Financial Officer, Treasurer, and one or more Vice Presidents. Any number of offices may be held by the same person.

**4.2 Appointment; Removal.** Officers are appointed by, serve at the pleasure of, and may be removed with or without cause by, the Board.

**4.3 Duties.** Officers shall have the powers and duties customarily incident to their offices and such other duties as the Board prescribes.

## ARTICLE V — STOCK

**5.1 Shares.** Shares of the Corporation may be certificated or uncertificated, as provided by resolution of the Board.

**5.2 Transfer.** Transfers of stock shall be made only on the books of the Corporation (or its transfer agent) by the holder of record or a duly authorized attorney, subject to applicable restrictions on transfer under federal and state securities laws.

**5.3 Transfer Agent.** The Board may appoint one or more transfer agents and registrars.

**5.4 Lost Certificates.** The Corporation may issue a new certificate (or uncertificated shares) in place of any certificate alleged to have been lost, stolen or destroyed, upon such terms and indemnity as the Board may require.

## ARTICLE VI — INDEMNIFICATION

To the fullest extent permitted by the DGCL, the Corporation shall indemnify and advance expenses to each director and officer made or threatened to be made a party to any proceeding by reason of service as a director or officer of the Corporation. Rights under this Article are contract rights and shall not be diminished retroactively.

## ARTICLE VII — GENERAL

**7.1 Fiscal Year.** The fiscal year of the Corporation shall end on {{company.fiscal_year_end}} unless otherwise fixed by the Board.

**7.2 Corporate Seal.** The Corporation may adopt a corporate seal, but the absence of a seal shall not affect the validity of any instrument.

**7.3 Notices; Electronic Transmission.** Notices to directors and stockholders may be given in writing or by electronic transmission to the extent permitted by the DGCL.

**7.4 Amendment.** These Bylaws may be adopted, amended or repealed by the Board or by the stockholders.
